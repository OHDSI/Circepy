### Cohort Entry Events

People may enter the cohort when observing any of the following:

1. condition occurrences of 'thrombophilia or APS'.

Restrict entry events to with any of the following criteria:

   1. having at least 1 measurement of 'Anti-beta2 glycoprotein 1 antibody', starting between 400 days before and 14 days after cohort entry start date; having at least 1 measurement of 'Anti-beta2 glycoprotein 1 antibody', starting between 365 days before and 90 days before 'Anti-beta2 glycoprotein 1 antibody' start date.
   2. having at least 1 measurement of 'Anticardiolipin antibody measurement', starting between 400 days before and 14 days after cohort entry start date; having at least 1 measurement of 'Anticardiolipin antibody measurement', starting between 365 days before and 90 days before 'Anticardiolipin antibody measurement' start date.
   3. having at least 1 measurement of 'Lupus anticoagulant assay', starting between 400 days before and 14 days after cohort entry start date; having at least 1 measurement of 'Lupus anticoagulant assay', starting between 365 days before and 90 days before 'Lupus anticoagulant assay' start date.
  

### Inclusion Criteria

#### 1. Anticoagulants 180 days after diagnosis

Entry events having at least 1 drug exposure of 'Anticoagulants indicated for APS', starting between 0 days before and 180 days after cohort entry start date.

Limit qualifying entry events to the all events per person.

### Cohort Exit

The person exits the cohort at the end of continuous observation.

### Cohort Eras

Remaining events will be combined into cohort eras if they are within 0 days of each other.
