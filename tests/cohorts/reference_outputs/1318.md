### Cohort Entry Events

People with continuous observation of 365 days before event may enter the cohort when observing any of the following:

1. condition occurrence of 'primary lung cancer' for the first time in the person's history.

Limit cohort entry events to the earliest event per person.

Restrict entry events to with the following event criteria: who are female or male, between 40 and 90 years old.  

### Inclusion Criteria

#### 1. (personal history of primary lung cancer) no personal history of primary lung cancer

Entry events with all of the following criteria:

   1. having no observations of 'personal history of lung cancer' (including 'personal history of lung cancer' source concepts), starting anytime on or before cohort entry start date; allow events outside observation period.
   2. having no observations of 'personal history of lung cancer', starting anytime on or before cohort entry start date; allow events outside observation period; with value as concept: "neoplasm of respiratory tract" or "malignant neoplasm of lower respiratory tract".


#### 2. (secondary) lung cancer (before)

Entry events having no measurements of 'metastasis to lung', starting anytime prior to cohort entry start date; allow events outside observation period.

#### 3. (secondary) lung cancer (on or before)

Entry events having no measurements of 'metastasis to lung', starting anytime on or before cohort entry start date; allow events outside observation period.

#### 6. no evidence of being SCLC (anytime): Not have a specific diagnosis of SCLC or treatment. The problem with this approach is minority of Primary Lung Cancer are SCLC.

   2. having no condition occurrences of 'small cell lung cancer', allow events outside observation period.
   3. having no drug exposures of 'drugs likely meant for small cell lung cancer', allow events outside observation period.
